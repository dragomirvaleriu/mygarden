# Soil Chemistry: pH, Macronutrients & Mineral Lockout

## pH: The Key That Opens or Locks Nutrients

Soil pH is the scale of acidity/alkalinity (1–14, neutral at 7). But in horticulture, its importance is not acidity per se, but **nutrient availability**. At the wrong pH, the fertilizers you apply remain chemically locked in the soil, inaccessible to roots.

**Optimal window for lawns:** pH 6.0 – 6.8

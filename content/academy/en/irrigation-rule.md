# The Golden Rule of Irrigation: Deep and Infrequent

## Root Physiology: Why Water Depth Matters

Lawn roots are adaptive structures — they grow **toward the water**. If you water briefly each day (5-10 minutes), water only penetrates 2-3 cm deep. Roots concentrate in this shallow layer, becoming completely dependent on artificial irrigation. At the first hot day or drought, this 2-3 cm layer evaporates in hours, and your lawn enters severe stress.

**Correct objective:** Force roots down to 10-15 cm, where temperature and moisture are stable even in heat waves.

## Watering Standards

| Season | Amount/m² | Frequency | Time |
|--------|----------|-----------|------|
| Spring | 10-15 L | 1x/week | 05:00-08:00 |
| Summer | 15-25 L | 2x/week | 05:00-07:00 |
| Autumn | 8-10 L | 1x/2 weeks | 06:00-09:00 |

## The Catch Can Test (Essential)

Before programming your irrigation controller, you must know **how fast your sprinkler deposits water**:

1. Place 3-4 metal cans or plastic cups in different spots of your irrigation zone.
2. Run the sprinkler for **exactly 15 minutes**.
3. Measure with a ruler how many **millimeters** of water collected in each can.
4. Average = your sprinkler's deposition rate in mm/15 min.

**Formula:** `(Desired liters / mm_collected_in_15_min) × 15 = Minutes needed`

## Evening Irrigation Fungal Syndrome

Watering between 17:00-22:00 is the **most common cause of fungal disease** in lawns. Wet leaf + warm summer night = 10-12 hours of perfect incubation for:

- **Brown Patch** (*Rhizoctonia solani*) — circular brown/yellow patches with green center
- **Pythium Blight** — watery patches appearing overnight
- **Dollar Spot** — small coin-sized patches, especially on N-deficient lawns

**Simple rule:** If you can't water in the morning, don't water that day.
